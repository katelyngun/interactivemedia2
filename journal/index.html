Overlay Design Pattern

Upon reading this article, I learned about the best practices for modals and how most of the time, modals can annoy users more than be helpful when not used right. Modals are mostly useful for getting user inputs or showing additional information on the page, but using them for other purposes can be frustrating for users.

They can be interruptive to a user’s experience and therefore must be selectively used within designs. Some tips I learned from the article for reducing the irritability of modals is making sure that they are easy to close, don’t take up your whole screen, and will only pop up after users allow them rather than at random.

I connected these new learnings with my own experiences with modals, and when I found them to be useful and necessary vs when they annoyed me. With this, I plan to use modals with intentionality moving forward.

Research Form Design

Reading this article, I learned many things about designing forms that I hadn’t previously thought of, but found to be very necessary from the perspective of a user who hates filling out forms.

These methods of creating forms help create a more accessible and efficient experience, especially as many users find forms to be long, complex, and daunting at times, making them not want to fill them out or feel confused when completing them.

I think important methods to practice include accessibility features for all users, such as allowing control over text size and not relying on color alone for communication. Form feedback is also very important, since I often have difficulty knowing what I entered wrong and dislike having to search through every field to find the mistake.

I was filling out this form last month for Film Freeway, a film festival submission website, and every field was very clear and concise. It followed many of the methods stated in the article, such as explaining why “Country of Origin” is required, using dropdown menus for long lists, organizing information vertically, and placing the most important fields at the top.
